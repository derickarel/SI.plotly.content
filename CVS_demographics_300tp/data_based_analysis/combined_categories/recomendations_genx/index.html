1. Elevate Positive Customer Service: Many customers have praised the store for its excellent customer service, describing staff as "professional," "helpful," and "nice." CVS should amplify this strength in its marketing materials, showcasing personalized testimonials such as "Misty in the salon was absolutely wonderful" to emphasize the high quality of its customer service.

  2. Highlight Cleanliness and Organization: Shoppers have also noticed and appreciate the store's cleanliness and organization. Marketing messaging should highlight this, creating a picture of the store as a pleasant and efficient shopping environment.

  3. Improve Shipping Services: A notable weakness that CVS needs to address is its shipping services, which customers have described as "absolutely horrible." The company should review its shipping practices and work to improve the quality of this service, then communicate these improvements to customers in its marketing materials.

  4. Improve Store Stock and Presentation: Customers have mentioned that shelves are often bare, and the store can be disorganized and dirty. CVS needs to address its stock and shop floor management issues to ensure a satisfying shopping experience for it's customers. Improvement in these areas should then be communicated effectively to reassure shoppers.

  5. Promote Use of Coupons and Loyalty Points: Customers have referenced the benefits of using coupons and loyalty points when shopping at CVS. The store should make better use of this in its marketing to attract budget-conscious shoppers or those looking for a good deal.

  6. Employee Training and Inclusivity: Some reviews mention employees' poor attitude or perceived discriminatory behavior. CVS needs to ensure it provides proper training and fosters an inclusive environment. Highlighting diversity and inclusivity principles in marketing materials can help reassure customers of CVS's commitment to a positive shopping experience for everyone.
