Marketing Report

Marketing Overview

 

Based on the provided data, areas of significant mention by CVS millennials customers were identified. The thematic analysis revealed that key concerns focused mostly on service quality. The issues raised included poor customer service like unanswered phone calls, erroneous prescription processing, and displeasure with the automated phone system.

 

'Label' elements such as "DONT BUY", "phone, hold, over - Terrible service", "Best studio and music store", "Hang up on", "Awful awful awful", "ready, called, day - Great service", "Horrible pharmacy", "Great experience in Eastchester br", and "Automated phone sucks" showed significant negative sentiment. This is a worrying trend as poor customer service may result in customer loss and damage the reputation of the brand.

 

The percentage variance explained for these groups ranged from 61.4% to 100% suggesting that these issues are the predominant reasons for customer dissatisfaction among the CVS Millennial customer base.

 

Marketing Recommendations

 

1. Improve Call Center Service
Given the large volume of negative reviews related to unanswered calls or automated phone systems, CVS should improve its call center services. This could involve hiring more personnel or improving training practices to ensure all calls are promptly and effectively addressed.

 

2. Enhance Prescription Processing System
Multiple customers mentioned issues with prescriptions not being ready on time, or even receiving wrong ones. Implementing a robust and efficient prescription processing system is vital to reduce these occurrences.

 

3. Personalized Customer Service
Millennials have been cited in numerous studies as valuing personalized service. As such, CVS should strive to create a customer experience that caters to individual needs. For instance, providing personalized product recommendations or health tips could help enhance customer satisfaction.

 

4. Improve Automated Phone System
The dissatisfaction with the automated phone system suggests a need for a review and possible overhaul of the system. Options could include simplifying the system, using more natural language processing technology, or providing clearer options for customers to talk to a human representative.

 

5. Invest in Staff Training
A number of reviews pointed out perceived incompetence or unhelpfulness among staff members. Investing in comprehensive staff training programs would not only improve employees' skills but also their ability to handle complex customer situations.

 

6. Utilize Digital Channels
Millennials are a digital-native generation which provides an opportunity for CVS to leverage digital channels for marketing and customer service. For instance, apps or online portals could be used to update customers on prescription status, provide health information or deal with queries. Integrating more digital solutions in their service operations could greatly appeal to this group.
