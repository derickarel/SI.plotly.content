Marketing Overview and Recommendations

Marketing Overview

 

The given data provides insights into areas of weaknesses that significantly impact CVS Store Experience Boomers (customers). The prominent issues are associated with drive thru services; proved by two labels which are mentioned significantly more: "Avoid at all cost" and "Terrible drive thru". These labels are associated with concerns and complaints related to long waiting times, slow service, closed drive through services, and poor customer service.

 

The area of "Avoid at all cost" explains around 0.44% of variance in the data while "Terrible drive thru" explains 0.21% of variance. Both tend to contribute negatively to the CVS Store experiences of the boomer population. It's apparent from the reviews that customers have had to experience long delays at drive-through services, often leading to them avoiding the service altogether.

 

Marketing Recommendations

 

1. Enhance Drive Thru Experience

The recurring issue of poor drive-thru service from customer feedback indicates a strong need to revamp this service. Staff training to increase efficiency and deploying an efficient system for handling the rush should be considered. A faster service speed at the drive-thru would mitigate customer frustration and improve overall ratings.

 

2. Maintain Operation Time Consistency

Customers have complained about the inconsistency of drive-thru service availability. Making sure the drive-thru service is available during all working hours would increase the store's reliability and customer satisfaction rate.

 

3. Improve intercommunication

It's observed that customers complain about not being acknowledged by the staff in the drive-thru. Deploying an intercom or a similar solution for better communication could reduce the frustration of waiting customers.

 

4. Customer Service Training

Several reviews indicate that staff behavior is often perceived as rude or inattentive. An active approach to training the staff for better customer handling could significantly improve customer experience, and thereby, ratings and reviews.

 

5. Increasing Drive Thru Lanes

If possible, consider expanding the number of drive-thru lanes. It could facilitate faster service especially during peak hours, improving the overall customer experience.

 

6. Evaluate Customer Feedback Regularly

Adopting a process to regularly review and act upon customer feedback (especially those from the boomer population) would aid in identifying and addressing concerns promptly. This process serves as a proactive way of improving the store experience.
