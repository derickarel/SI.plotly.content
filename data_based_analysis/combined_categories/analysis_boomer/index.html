The data provides marketing insights into CVS's strengths and weaknesses in terms of performance, divided into two groups: Baby Boomers and younger generations. Baby Boomers have positively rated CVS for its good customer service and rewarding program and negative interactions are related to long phone hold times, mishandling medications, and poor customer service. Thus, CVS should boost their strengths by marketing its customer-centric approach and rewarding program specifically targeting this group. Further, it could improve relations with this group by way of enhancing telephone customer services and ensuring better management of medicinal stocks. On the other hand, younger generations have praised the Pleasurable shopping experience but reported issues with the drive-through service, signifying a need for its improvement. Marketing efforts to younger generations can be focused on the enjoyable shopping experience while also working to resolve issues in the drive-through service. By understanding these strengths and weaknesses, CVS can direct its marketing and operational strategies in a way that helps address the different needs of Baby Boomers and younger generations.
