1. Encourage and Promote Excellent Customer Service: Analyzed data reveals that customers highly value a positive and friendly service. Recognitions like "Great CS from Margaret" and "Thank, team, amazing" were commonly found in customer reviews. Staff members who gain prominence for excellent service can be highlighted in marketing campaigns, giving a personal touch to customer communications and encouraging new customers to visit for a superior service experience.

  2. Focus on Cleanliness and Well-Stocked Environment: One of CVS's appreciated strengths is cleanliness and well-stocked shelves. Phrases like "Clean and well stocked" and "Everything I needed" were emphasized in customer reviews. Publicizing cleanliness protocols and fully stocked environment in marketing materials can instill trust and attract customers who prioritize a neat shopping space.

  3. Address Complaints About Delay and Inefficiency: From reviews, it was revealed that customers found phone hold times, drive-thru service speed and readiness of medicines problematic. Common complaints include "Phone hold times are horrible," "Slow drive thru," and "Never have meds ready poor customer service." It is essential to improve these service areas and highlight these enhancements in marketing communications to assure customers that their feedback is highly valued and improvements have been made.

  4. Reiterate Value for Money: There were complaints about prices such as "Everything double regular price" and "Price gouging." CVS could reaffirm its commitment to affordability in its marketing communications, emphasizing promotional offers, discounts, or loyalty programs to ensure potential customers of its value for money.

  5. Boost Confidence in Product Offering: One of CVS's strengths mentioned by customers was a wide variety of grocery and frozen food products. Comments included "Good for shopping grocery also frozen food from." Highlighting the product range in marketing communications, with emphasis on quality and diversity, can attract potential customers looking for a one-stop-shop solution.

  6. Improve Language and Cultural Sensitivity Training: Some reviews indicated dissatisfaction with staff language skills or cultural sensitivity, such as "My dad was denied service just because he speak fluent English." CVS can revise its language and cultural sensitivity training and highlight this commitment in its marketing messaging to assure customers that CVS values inclusivity and respects diversity.
