Based on the data analysis, it is clear that CVS's strengths lie in the superior quality and effectiveness of its products which is significantly appreciated by its clientele. High percentage labels such as "I love this product," "Really good," and "Best cleanser ever" show the affinity of customers for CVS's product offerings. Characteristic reviews provided additional insight into these strengths, revealing that customers particularly value the product's texture, their skin's feel after use, and experiential aspects like scent. These attributes have helped CVS create a strong, preferred brand among its shoppers and stronger customer loyalty.

However, the data also offered an understanding of the significant weaknesses that CVS needs to address. CVS shoppers were emphatic about their dissatisfaction with labels such as "Don't waste your" and "Broke me out". These labels accompanied by high weakness percentages signify a notable area of customer discontentment. Characteristic reviews further attested to these weaknesses with common complaints surrounding ineffectiveness, adverse skin reactions, and product-related breakouts. Market implications suggest that these areas warrant immediate attention from CVS to prevent further customer dissatisfaction, potential erosion in market share, and harm to the brand's reputation.
