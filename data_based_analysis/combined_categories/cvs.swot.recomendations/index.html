1. Capitalize on the love for the product expressed by CVS shoppers. Numerous testimonials talk about how much they love various products, such as the ones that give a dewy glow to the skin, or lip masks that keep the lips hydrated. Implement campaigns that highlight such beloved products and emphasize how they can provide the benefits customers are praising. These tactics will not only retain current CVS shoppers but also have the potential to attract competitors' customers.

 

2. Address the issue of customers' allergic reactions to certain products. Many customer reviews mentioned instances of allergic reactions, skin breaking out, or redness. As such, provide clearer instructions for patch testing products, or consider highlighting hypoallergenic products. Enhancing customer service to handle such incidents could also improve customer satisfaction and prevent losing customers to competitors.

 

3. CVS must work on improving the perceived quality of their products. A recurring theme in the weaknesses is that some customers view CVS's products as a waste of money, not worth their price, or not doing anything special. Utilize marketing strategies that highlight the usefulness and effectiveness of product lines, and consider further investing in product development to ensure customer satisfaction and the avoidance of potential negative reviews.

 

4. Improve the scents and smells of products, as some seem to cause displeasure among users. Invest in product reformulation to make them more appealing. Additionally, consider sampling opportunities in-store so customers can experience the scent before purchasing - this would increase customer satisfaction and decrease the chance of returns.

 

5. Emphasize products that have created significant improvements for users. Reviews mention that some products have helped clear acne, provided hydration, and created glowing skin. Highlight such transformative "before and after" experiences in marketing media to showcase product effectiveness, encouraging potential customers to make a purchase.

 

6. Prioritize sustainability and label transparency as a marketing strategy. Some customers have expressed concern over certain products containing polyethylene, which can be harmful to the environment. Being open about product ingredients and making a commitment to reducing environmental impact could attract conscious shoppers and give CVS an advantage over competitors.
