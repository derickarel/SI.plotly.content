Marketing Report

Marketing Overview

 

The data shows that the labels with the worst performances across all segments are significantly associated with negative customer experiences, such as “Waste of Money,” “Horrible Customer Service,” “Terrible Customer Service,” “Slow” and other labels implying dissatisfaction. Furthermore, the detailed reviews indicate common issues mainly related to customer service inefficiency, particularly from the pharmacies, reflected in complaints of waiting times, unanswered phones, and poor management. This underperformance is widespread among all four groups extracted from the data.

 

The percent variance explained for each group, which ranges from approximately 39% to 28%, suggests that these issues account for a substantial proportion of the poor KPIs. It is therefore critical that these areas are addressed in future marketing strategies.

 

Marketing Recommendations

 

1. Improve Customer Service: Negative reviews are predominantly centered around poor customer service, particularly in the pharmacy area. Regular training programs should be implemented to improve the staff's customer interaction skills, focusing on courtesy, empathy, and active listening. Strategies should also include measures to reduce waiting times and speed up services.

 

2. Effective Communication: Frequently reported issues involve poor telephone communication, with many complaints about unanswered calls and disorganization. A more reliable and efficient phone system should be implemented, ensuring queries are attended to promptly. Customer Service Representatives should also be trained to handle multiple queries quickly and efficiently.

 

3. Randing Improvement: Reviews suggest a tarnished reputation and need for brand repair. A multi-channel marketing campaign focused on reintroducing the brand's values and mission, emphasizing improvements, could contribute towards restoring the company's image.

 

4. Implementation of a Customer Feedback System: Regular gathering and analysis of customer feedback may help identify immediate issues before they escalate, and provide more understanding of customer's needs.

 

5. Promotions and Rewards: Implementing promotional offers and loyalty rewards can help restore trust and attract back dissatisfied customers. Loyal customers should be identified and rewarded with exclusive deals or priority service.

 

6. Collaboration with Healthcare Providers: Given the negative perception of the pharmacy sector, partnerships with healthcare providers for integrated service delivery could enhance customer engagement and satisfaction. This collaboration might include health talks, free health check-ups, or in-store clinics.
