Marketing Overview

The given data provides an incisive view into the issues faced by millennials engaging with Walgreens' services, particularly where weaknesses are noticed. The defined weaknesses and their corresponding percentage variations have been led by four key issues which have been defined as: "Waiting in line", "phone, hold, over - horrible service", "Horrible", and "hold, phone, call - Terrible customer service".

These issues point to significant service bottlenecks impacting customer satisfaction, particularly in areas of store speed ("Waiting in line", a deficiency noticed by a full 100% of the variance group), customer support ("phone, hold, over - horrible service", with 93.86% noticing), direct service quality ("Horrible", noticed by 78.94%), and telephonic customer service ("hold, phone, call - Terrible customer service", a weakness noticed by 77.89%).

Marketing Recommendations

1. Enhance In-Store Experience: Observing that the primary point of contention for millennials appears to be the time spent waiting in line, efforts must be made to improve in-store processes to make the check-out process faster. This could involve employing more staff during peak hours, incorporating self-checkout stations, or using technology solutions to expedite payment.

2. Improve Customer Support: It's seen that millennials have had negative experiences with phone call-based customer support. In this digital age, making customer support more seamless and available through various platforms (like chatbots on the website, a robust FAQ section, or active social media responses) might improve customer satisfaction.

3. Enhance Service Quality: The term 'horrible' is quite broad, but it often refers to overall customer experience - incorporating elements like staff behavior, product availability, and store ambiance. Regular staff training on customer interaction, ensuring optimal stocking of products, and maintaining a clean, welcoming store ambiance can go a long way towards rectifying this.

4. Robust Telephonic Support: Many customers have pointed out poor telephonic services. The necessity of having robust telephonic support, particularly during times when online services fail, or personal assistance is required, remains important. Ensuring that telephonic customer service is improved, with reduced waiting times and friendly, helpful representatives could be beneficial.

5. Leverage Technology: Walgreens could leverage technology to improve their overall customer service. Features such as online live tracking of prescription readiness, automated systems for faster checkout, online customer support etc., could significantly improve the customer experience and broaden the company's appeal to tech-savvy millennials.

6. Encourage Feedback: Encouraging customer feedback and acting up on them is essential for continuous improvement. Tools to enable easy feedback through various touchpoints like the physical store, online website, and social media platforms can provide invaluable insights into areas that need improvement. This will not only help solve immediate issues but result in proactive discovery and rectification of potential problems.
