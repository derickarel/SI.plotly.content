Marketing Overview and Recommendations

Marketing Overview

The provided data indicates an overall dissatisfaction experienced by the Gen X consumers at CVS store. This is mainly due to perceived quality issues with products and services, policy issues related to auto refills, accessibility, and customer service interactions.

 

Issues such as 'Horrible pharmacy', 'Incompetent', and 'DO NOT BUY' contribute to a negative image and customer experience for CVS. Despite this, CVS has positive appeal for consumers with statements like 'ready, called, day - Great service', 'Great place to buy', 'My favorite money saving', and 'I love my CVS PH'.

 

The most dissatisfaction is related to the pharmacy service followed by the customer service and auto-refill policies. Responses indicate that the marketing implication for these data groups involves addressing the areas of weaknesses in order to improve the perceived value of the services provided by CVS.

 

The discovery of these weaknesses present an opportunity for CVS to improve their services which could ultimately enhance customer satisfaction, increase revneue, and improve the overall brand image.

 

Marketing Recommendations

1. CVS must improve service at the pharmacy. This is significantly mentioned in the survey as an area where customers feel let down, and hence some focus should be shifted to this area. There should be continuous staff training to help improve their competence in managing customers with empathy while ensuring accurate dispensing of medication. Furthermore, proper systems should be installed to ensure that all medications are available as and when needed by customers. This will help address the ‘Horrible pharmacy’ and ‘Incompetent’ label, and bring considerable change in the way customers perceive CVS.

 

2. The 'ready, called, day - Great service' label suggests that the provision of timely information to the customers is an important factor. To meet this need, CVS should implement reliable and efficient communication channels to keep customers informed within suitable timeframes especially when it comes to the readiness of prescriptions.

 

3. CVS must also address the issue around 'Auto refills are'. Poor refill policies can result in customers not being able to access important medications when they are due, resulting in health repercussions. CVS should therefore revamp its refill policies along with all other related procedures, making them more streamlined and customer friendly.

 

4. Data indicates dissatisfaction with customer service interactions. CVS should consider improving personnel and resource allocation to enhance their customer service quality. This could involve hiring of more dedicated, experienced staff and their training to provide quality customer experiences.

 

5. In terms of product availability, statements like 'Great place to buy' and 'My favorite money saving' suggest that customers appreciate the range and prices of products. However, the negative implications such as 'DO NOT BUY' and 'Avoid at all cost' imply issues with product quality or experience. Therefore, CVS should ensure quality assurance processes are implemented and consistently reviewed in order to maintain high-quality standards for products available.

 

6. Finally, given that consumers mentioned 'I love my CVS PH', CVS can consider recognising and reinforcing positive experiences in order to reproduce them across all their stores and build a decent customer loyalty system that rewards loyal customers. Encouraging loyal customers to share their positive experiences can also serve as a tool for CVS to attract new customers.
