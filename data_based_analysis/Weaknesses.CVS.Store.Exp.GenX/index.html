Marketing Recommendations

1. Improve Pharmaceutical Services: Reading from the frequent mentions of 'horrible pharmacy' and related phrases, investing in pharmacy staff training for better customer interactions, timely medication deliveries, and error-free prescription handling is necessary.

2. Streamline Communication Channels: Given the apparent dissatisfaction with communication services such as text alerts and calling - it's advisable to streamline these channels for effective and timely notifications. This could include automating refill reminders or text alerts when prescriptions are ready to pick up.

3. Evaluate Pricing Strategies: It's evident from phrases like 'Avoid at all cost' and 'Greedy price gouging', that price is a significant concern among consumers. CVS should consider evaluating and adjusting their pricing strategies to be more competitive and acceptable among GenX consumers.

4. Improve Inventory Management: Restocking popular products more efficiently can help keep customer loyalty. Stock unavailability seems to be a significant pain point among GenX consumers and could be improved by better inventory management.

5. Enhance Customer Service: From complaints like 'Incompetent', CVS should focus on enhancing customer service by training staff on customer relationship management, improving response times to complaints, and ensuring customer needs are promptly addressed.

6. Optimize Wait Times: CVS should invest in optimization techniques to reduce customer wait times in stores and online platforms. This can be achieved by upgrading checkout systems or employing more staff during peak hours.
